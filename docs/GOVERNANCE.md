# DarkLens V0.2 — Governance

## Core design principle
> DarkLens detects and documents evidence of potentially manipulative interface
> patterns. It does not determine legal liability.

Every output surface (badge, side panel, any future export) must preserve this
distinction. "Potentially relevant to [instrument]" is acceptable language;
"violates [instrument]" is not, anywhere in this codebase.

## Regulatory mapping methodology
- The lookup table (`src/governance/lookupTable.js`) is **static and hand-curated**,
  not retrieved by a RAG pipeline or generated by a model. This was a deliberate
  scope decision: a hand-curated table is 100% auditable and eliminates the
  hallucinated-citation risk of a retrieval pipeline, at the cost of not scaling
  automatically to new jurisdictions — an acceptable tradeoff for V0.2's 3
  detectors and 1 primary jurisdiction.
- Every entry cites its source document, a retrieval date, and a last-verified date
  (see `docs/sources/`). Laws and guidance are amended over time; an artifact that
  cannot say when its legal text was last checked is itself a governance gap.
- If a detector's `patternId` has no entry in the table, the system must display
  "No matching provision found" — never a fabricated or inferred citation.
- **Primary jurisdiction:** India's CCPA Guidelines for Prevention and Regulation of
  Dark Patterns, 2023, issued under Section 18 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.
  This is the anchor jurisdiction because it is the most directly relevant to the
  regulatory-analyst roles this project is built to demonstrate readiness for.

## Simulated Ethics Board Review

*A short, honest memo anticipating the questions a review board would actually ask.*

**Q: How do you prevent DarkLens from being used to learn how to evade detection?**
V0.2's detection logic is open-source by design (this is a portfolio project), so
"security through obscurity" was never a real option and isn't claimed as one — see
`RED-TEAM.md`, which explicitly tests evasion via paraphrasing and obfuscation
rather than assuming the detector's logic stays secret.

**Q: What if the detector flags a legitimate, non-manipulative element?**
This is why every finding carries an Evidence Tier (1–4) rather than a flat
confidence score, and why Tier 3 ("Contextual Ambiguity") exists as an explicit
"flag for human review" category instead of forcing a binary yes/no. The system is
designed to say "unclear" rather than guess.

**Q: Could this tool itself become a vector for reputational harm?**
Yes, if misused — which is why the System Card explicitly prohibits naming real
companies in any published material from this project, and why every governance
mapping is phrased as "potentially relevant," never "violates."

**Q: Why rule-based detection instead of an LLM for V0.2?**
A rule-based system is fully inspectable — every finding can be traced to an exact
regex or DOM check. An LLM classifier introduces both a new attack surface (indirect
prompt injection from webpage content) and a new evaluation burden (bias, hallucinated
rationale) that V0.2 isn't scoped to responsibly carry. That tradeoff is deferred to
a clearly-labeled V2, not hidden inside a V0.2 that implies more sophistication than
it has.
